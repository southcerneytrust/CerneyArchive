Parish Boundary Locations 14 & 15               

Anglo-Saxon 

English
14. Up andlang Cyrne thaet on an Lide Lace.

15.  Andlang thaere Laece thaet eft on Cates Stan thaer hit aer onfang.

 14.  Up along the Churn then to a slow stream in a Dingle.

15.  Along the Slow Stream then again to Cat's Stone where it first began.

 

 

 

 

If the text has been correctly transcribed then this must refer to a local name for part of the Churn near the N end of the E boundary.  Had it cut a deep course at this point?
