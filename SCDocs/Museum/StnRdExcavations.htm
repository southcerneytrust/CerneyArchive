During excavations for the building of a large garden store, in May 1999, significant evidence of occupation through the centuries was revealed. Since the house, in Station Road, was known to have been built c 1600, this was not entirely surprising.

The wall from the north

The posthole (now alas destroyed).

 

What, however, was completely unexpected was clear and significant evidence of domestic occupation going back to the 11th century. A posthole was found some 2 metres away from a substantial piece of medieval walling. The wall had fragmentary evidence of a pebble floor on one side – the rest had been destroyed by a 19th century privy! The medieval complex could be dated, roughly, by a very large quantity of soot blackened fragments of cooking pot immediately beside the wall. It was clear that, by chance, the cooking area or the rubbish pit beside it, had been located. The pottery, some of it rather fine, was easily identifiable and suggests the area was in use from c 1066 to at least 1400.

The upper levels showed that even after this area had gone out of use there continued to be domestic occupation close by since pottery from every century, in some quantity was found.
