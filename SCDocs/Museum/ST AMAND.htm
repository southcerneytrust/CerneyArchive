South Cerney Exhibition


Miles of Gloucester gave land to the de Verdon family. In 1231 their large manor in the parish passed to a son-in-law, Almaric de Sancto Amando. He and his mother-in-law both had houses in Cerney which they then exchanged!

 

The family held many manors. They were great courtiers and soldiers: variously Governor of Bordeaux, godfather to the King's son, Justiciar of Ireland, commanders of royal armies abroad. Extraordinarily we even have a 'portrait' of one: when still young he was depicted, with the King, Hugh Despenser and other great courtiers, as mourners on a memorial brass.

 

 

 

 

The St. Amand's have left a permanent mark on the village. In 1278 they were allowed to move the road to enclose a fish stew (fish breeding pond) within their grounds. The earthworks of their manor, the remains of the fishery and, obvious to every motorist, the bend in the road still remain.
