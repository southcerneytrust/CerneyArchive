South Cerney Exhibition


 

 

 

 

In 983 Aelfhere, Ealdorman of Mercia, died and his brother-in-law, Aelfric Cild, was appointed his successor. Almost at once Aelfhere's widow, Eadflaed, accused Aelfric of seizing some of her estates, including Cerney.

 

A council of King Aethelred and 'all the best men of the kingdom' met at Cirencester. Aelfric was exiled, perhaps more because of a charge of treason, and Eadflaed had her estates restored on condition they passed, on her death, to the monastery at Abingdon.

 

In 999, perhaps to clarify the legacy, two charters were issued describing the earlier events, listing the witnesses, a remarkable collection of the great and the good, and defining the boundaries of Cerney, unchanged to this day.
