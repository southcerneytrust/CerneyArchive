SOUTH CERNEY PRE-SCHOOL PLAYGROUP

South Cerney Pre-School is a playgroup set in a rural community and caters for children aged 2years 10 months until their entry into school. It has been in existence since 1969, but moved to its present site of Ann Edwards School in 1988.

  

Though it maintains close links with the school ï¿½ particularly the reception-staff, the Pre-School is an independently run, charitable organization and relies upon the management of a volunteer committee of parents who not only deal with the administration and maintenance, but have also to generate funds through various activities in order to finance the Group.

There are currently 55 children in attendance within the Group, and 5 permanent members of staff, three of whom are present each day.We are also assisted by all parents on a rota-basis.

Our day begins at 9.00am when the children can choose an activity from a variety prepared before they arrive. At 9.15 we all meet for registration and a talk about our Interest Table, the theme of which changes weekly. Play then continues until 10.30, when the session becomes more structured, with a small group undertaking an activity more directly aimed at writing or maths., and the remainder enjoying a story.After this it is snack-time, then a more physical activity, usually outside, before we have a Group activity, such as music, prior to home time at 11.45am.

 

Ours is a very flexible day, but using a wide range of activities we aim to meet the six Desirable Learning Outcomes specified by the School Curriculum Assessment Authority. Thus we endeavour to give the children a wide Knowledge and Understanding of the World around them by introducing various topics and new experiences. We help their Personal and Social Development by encouraging independence, sensitivity and co-operation with others in the Group. We introduce them to Language and Literacy by providing books, stories, rhymes and opportunities to develop word recognition and early writing skills. For Maths we use a variety of sorting, matching and counting activities. Their Development is encouraged with the range of large and small equipment, both inside and out, and they are given many opportunities for Creative Development through various mediums such as paint, collage, pens, junk-modelling, clay, Playdough, woodwork and construction toys. Our success in meeting these criteria was evident in the excellent report we received after our recent Ofsted inspection.

Overall we aim to produce a happy, safe and stimulating experience for young children which will be a sound basis for their entry into school.
