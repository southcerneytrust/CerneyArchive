The responses to the first sections of the questionnaires produced several interesting facts. Maybe not too surprising, the first statistic is an equal division of males and females and, apart from the late teenage/early 20's years, there is an even spread of age groups between 11 and 80.

Approximately one fifth of the villagers were born in the village, whilst just over half spent most of their childhood more than 30 miles away. Nearly three quarters of us have lived in the parish for less than 15 years. Each household averages ownership of just over one car.

 

The majority are happy with their home - the main discontent being that it is either too small, or there are not enough bedrooms. A fifth of us feel there is need for starter or young people’s homes.


The polling for a housing "status quo" was strong with 84% wanting the increase in housing stock to be kept below 20 dwellings over the next 10 years.


Giving a strong indicator to the growth of the village over the past 40 years, three quarters of our homes have been built since 1951, whilst 36 are pre 1700 and 50 each from the 18th and 19th centuries.
