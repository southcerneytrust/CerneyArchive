Although some of the services listed are not available in the village, the majority of people had no difficulty reaching them, and the facilities within the village received a very favourable response. The only significant problem was the location of hospitals at Swindon and Cheltenham – more than seventy people had some difficulty in reaching them.

A high level of appreciation was expressed for the quality of service in this section over the past five years and, with the exception of the home help service, very few thought the provision of the services to be poor.

The use over the past five years of the loan of medical equipment, maternity services, meals on wheels and an optician, the majority although small in number, were impressed.
