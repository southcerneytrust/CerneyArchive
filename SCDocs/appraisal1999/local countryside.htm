The responses in this section were both heart-felt and wide-ranging, but, importantly, villagers are positive about the Cotswold Water Park in its present state and for its future development in low-key tourism and recreation.

 

From the figures for campsite and hotel development it appears day-stay visitors should be encouraged and the need for a visitor / interpretation centre was highlighted.

 

  

 

Questions were included on footpath and bridleway usage, together with the desired location of cycle paths. Walking is easily the most popular leisure activity and 40 % like to cycle. This latter group feels that it is a poor relation and the need for cycle paths linking Cirencester and Keynes Country Park is highlighted, as well as alongside the Spine Road.

On the issue of the River Churn the majority felt that water meters would have little effect on the danger of drying up every summer, whilst, despite adverse press coverage, many felt the canal restoration worthwhile along with the old railway embankment. But the major issue in this section was litter and the need to bin it - better still its absence in the first place.

Efforts to improve the village visually are mainly targeted at individual households and the Parish Council, but the figures for the playing field, industrial and business areas indicate scope for improvement.

Although there was a 25% ‘yes’ for volunteers for local environmental schemes, there was only a handful of offers on the last page of the appraisal and these have been forwarded to the relevant village society.


It emerges that cyclists are annoying pedestrians and should be on the road, and the figures for traffic noise are before the Latton  and Cirencester bypasses and would surely now read differently. 

 

Finally in this section, dogs, or rather their owners, should be more considerate and use the 'poop scoop' bins.
