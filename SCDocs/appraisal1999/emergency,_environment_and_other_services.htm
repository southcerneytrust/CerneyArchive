In this section criticism of the ambulance, fire, police, postal and telephone services was invited.

Concerning ambulance and fire services, the majority had no opinion because presumably they had fortunately never required them, and only a handful thought they were poor.

The non-emergency police service provided an equally divided response of good, poor and no opinion, whereas with the emergency service over half had no opinion and 15% thought it poor. A substantial number showed concern at the absence of a "policeman on the beat". Possibly this is an argument for greater use of  special constables.

A high percentage were satisfied with the postal and domestic telephone systems, but to counter any complacency a substantial 15% were dissatisfied with the former.  the public telephone situation is rather different with only a third thinking it good to reasonable, a half with no opinion and 17% poor.
