Buses - the majority never use them, but a substantial 350 thought them essential and over 200 felt they were important.

Any improvement by the bus companies would still not attract half of the villagers, although the remainder said they would use them if the services were more frequent in the evenings. The most popular improvement would be lower fares.

The condition of our roads was generally regarded as reasonable by 56% and good to excellent by one fifth.  A large majority considered street parking hazardous.
