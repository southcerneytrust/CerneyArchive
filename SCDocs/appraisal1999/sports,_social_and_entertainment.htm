The vast majority list walking as their main outdoor leisure activity and nearly half of us enjoy cycling, - confirming the findings of a previous section. Water sports, tennis, angling and  football enjoyed equal popularity with the remainder

An interesting statistic was the presence of archery at the head of the list for outdoor leisure if made available.  The modern pastime of roller- blading was second in popularity followed by the old French tradition of boules.

 

As befits our area, water sports was voted easily the most oustanding for the excellence of facilities, with tennis coming way back in second place.

On the need for a sports pavilion on the playing field, comfortably more than half said "yes", but just over a third showed no interest.

And, on the question of the childrens play-areas, opinions were divided equally between reasonable, poor, very poor and no opinion - ninety people thought them good and just 14 folk felt they were excellent.

Nearly a third felt the need for a community centre in addition to the village hall, whilst over half ha d no opinion or thought it unnecessary.  Less than a fifth thought it was needed to replace the present hall.

Finally in this section, " Keep-Fit",  followed by badminton emerged as a clear favourite of the possible indoor activities.
