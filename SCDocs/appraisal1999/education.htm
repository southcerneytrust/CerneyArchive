Pre-school education is provided through the long established South Cerney Pre-school Playgroup.

A modern, purpose built building, constructed in the early 1990s, houses Ann Edwards School, a voluntary controlled Co-educational Church of England Primary School. The present site was occupied in 1972, vacating school buildings built in 1830 in School Lane and financed by a prominent South Cerney benefactor, Ann Edwards. The school provides state primary  education for approximately 300 children, aged from 4 to 11 years, drawn from the communities of South Cerney, Cerney Wick, Somerford Keynes and 29 Regt.

Secondary level state education for the youngsters of the village is mainly obtained in Cirencester at Kingshill School, Deer Park School and Cirencester College.

There are no private education facilities in the village, although some children do attend private and other state nurseries, primary and secondary schools in Cirencester, Cricklade, Fairford, Stroud, Gloucester and Cheltenham, and surrounding villages.

The committee recognised the importance of the school and education provision to the village. However we did not include any questions on education in the appraisal questionnaire since after much deliberation we felt unable to pose any question that would generate significant or  meaningful data.
