On Tuesday 13t October the Rev. Calvert visited our class. The church is eleven hundred years old and it's where people go to have their children christened. The child goes in a font and has water put on his head. Funerals are when people die and they are put in a fire or you can be buried under the ground. Weddings are when people get married. The vicar wears a white cassock, a dog collar, a girdle and a stole for christenings, weddings and communion. A communion service is when people have bread and wine. They have Easter, Harvest and Christmas services too.

Scott Brandreth

 

 

On Tuesday 13th October the Rev. Calvert came to show us what he wears for services. He wears a white cassock, a girdle and a stole. He wears these things for weddings and christenings. In Communion he gives out wafers or bread and wine. He puts the bread on a paten and he pours the wine into a chalice.

Sebastian Genillard

 

 

On Tuesday 13th October the Rev. Calvert visited our class. He came to tell us about the church and the services he has there. The church is over a thousand years old. It is a cross from a bird's eye view. At the end of the guttering there is a gargoyle to frighten evil spirits away. To tell the time they look at a sundial on the outside of the church.

Stacey Bumphrey

 

The head and foot are part of a wooden cross from All hallows Church. They are what were left over after Oliver Cromwell's soldiers were burning all the wooden statues and this is the only one left in the country.

Joseph Barry
